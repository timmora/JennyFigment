Bake Pinkerdoodles with Flora!

Let's make Flora Margaret's famous pink cinnamon cookies! Ask a grown-up to help!

Download PDF Version
Ask a grown-up for help!

This recipe uses an oven. Always ask a parent or guardian before you start baking!

1

Gather Your Ingredients

→
First, let's make sure we have everything we need! Ask a grown-up to help you find these in the kitchen:
  • 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) butter, softened
  • 1½ cups sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons cinnamon sugar (for rolling)
  • A few drops of pink food coloring
Science Fact! Cream of tartar is an acid that makes cookies chewy instead of crispy. That's the secret to the perfect Pinkerdoodle texture!
2

Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt. Set this bowl aside — we'll use it again in a moment!
Science Fact! Baking soda is a base and cream of tartar is an acid. When they meet in the oven, they react and create tiny bubbles — that's what makes your cookies rise and get fluffy!
3

Cream the Butter & Sugar

In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and sugar together until it's light and fluffy — about 3 minutes. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each one. Now add the pink food coloring! Mix until you get a pretty pink color.
Science Fact! Creaming butter and sugar traps tiny air bubbles inside. Those air bubbles expand in the oven's heat and make your cookies light and soft!
4

Combine & Chill

Slowly stir the dry ingredients into the pink butter mixture. Mix until just combined — don't over-mix! Cover the dough and put it in the refrigerator for 30 minutes. Ask a grown-up for help with the next step after chilling!
5

Roll & Coat

Preheat the oven to 375°F (ask a grown-up to do this!). Roll the dough into 1-inch balls. Then roll each ball in the cinnamon sugar mixture until it's completely coated. Place them on a greased baking sheet, 2 inches apart.
6

Bake & Enjoy!

Bake for 8–10 minutes, until the edges are lightly golden. Ask a grown-up to handle the oven! Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before eating. They'll be soft in the middle and slightly crispy on the outside — just like Flora Margaret makes them!

You made Pinkerdoodles!

Flora Margaret is so proud of you! Share them with someone you love!
