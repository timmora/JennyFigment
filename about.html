The Story of

Jennifer Hayes

"Use your imagination!"

Jennifer had a whimsical childhood during the 1970s and 80s, with imaginary friends and a trusty noble steed as her constant companions. As she grew older, her imagination and love for children’s books remained, guiding her to become a teacher. She’s now on an extraordinary adventure of her own into the world of children’s literature.

She is the author of The Extraordinary Imagination of Jenny Figment (January 5, 2024), Andy's Space Friends (September 2, 2024), and The Riddle of the Fairytale Village (October 19, 2025). Jennifer loves animals, especially pocket dragons, and has a passion for baking pinkerdoodles. She lives in Illinois with her husband, daughter, one cat, and two dogs—who sadly do not talk (though sometimes Jennifer imagines they do).

The Mission

Why I Write

I write because every child deserves to see themselves as extraordinary. Imagination is the most powerful force in the universe — and I want every young reader to know they already have it.

Growing up, Jennifer was the kind of kid who saw adventures in every corner of her imagination. She wrote stories in the margins of her notebooks, invented characters on long car rides, and could spend hours lost in a good book. When she began writing for children, she wanted to capture that same sense of wonder — and pass it on to a new generation.

The Jenny Figment series was born from a simple belief: that stories can change how children see themselves and the world. Every book Jennifer writes is a love letter to curious, creative, extraordinary kids everywhere.
